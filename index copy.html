PhD Candidate & Robotics Engineer

Sensorless Compliance.

PhD researcher at the Robotics and Mechatronics Laboratory (Takesue Lab) at Tokyo Metropolitan University. Specializing in Sensorless Adaptive Compliance Control, I focus on enabling redundant manipulators to interact safely using high-speed dynamics in C++ 20 and ROS 2. My academic research is grounded by my industrial role as a Robot Assembly Engineer (Intern) at Telexistence Inc., where I contribute to the high-precision assembly and hardware validation of Ghost Team robotic systems. This combination of theoretical control and hands-on mechanical engineering ensures my research is built on a first-principles understanding of real-world robotic hardware.
