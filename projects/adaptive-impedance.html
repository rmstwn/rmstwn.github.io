Adaptive Impedance
Control Architecture.

Developing an attention-based gain modulation framework for contact-rich assembly tasks using Kinova Gen3 Lite manipulators.

01. Abstract

Traditional impedance control often fails in unpredictable environments due to rigid gain scheduling. My research introduces a **dynamic gain-tuning model** that utilizes a Multi-Head Attention mechanism to interpret environmental interaction. By adjusting stiffness in real-time, we achieve a balance between tracking precision and physical safety during industrial assembly.

02. Mathematical Objective

$$ M(q)\ddot{x} + D(q, \dot{q}, \alpha)\dot{e} + K(q, \alpha)e = F_{ext} $$

Where \(\alpha\) represents the attention weights derived from the interaction force history. The controller minimizes contact force $F_{ext}$ while maintaining a target impedance profile $M, D, K$.

03. Methodology

1

Attention-Based Gain Tuning

The model processes proprioceptive data (joint torque/velocity) to predict the optimal compliance level. This prevents the "chatter" typically found in high-gain PD controllers.

2

Pinocchio RNEA Implementation

Implemented in C++ using the **Pinocchio** library for 1kHz real-time performance. This enables the calculation of the Recursive Newton-Euler Algorithm for inverse dynamics without sacrificing computational bandwidth.
